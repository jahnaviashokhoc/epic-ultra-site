### Section 1: The Challenge

**Tag:** The Challenge
**Headline:** AI in High-Risk Environments
**Subtext:** Energy trading organizations operate in a world of accelerating volatility, fragmented systems, and exponential data growth. AI promises intelligence and automation — but when deployed without proper architecture, it can amplify operational weaknesses instead of solving them.

**Problem Cards:**

1. **Fragmented Systems**
   Fragmented ETRM, ERP, and settlement systems create inconsistent data across operational platforms.

2. **No Governance Layer**
   AI models deployed without governance controls. Off-the-shelf AI lacks mandate controls, role-based limits, and financial guardrails — creating unacceptable risk at every decision point.

3. **Amplified Errors**
   Market volatility increases exposure sensitivity. Operational automation amplifies errors at scale.

4. **Black-Box Decisions**
   When AI journals an entry or flags a position, you need a full audit trail. Opaque models cannot survive regulatory scrutiny.

5. **Hallucination at Scale**
   Unstructured retrieval causes confabulation. In margin calculations and risk reports, one wrong number creates systemic exposure.

**Closing line:** Modern enterprises need more than AI tools. They need AI architecture built for controlled execution.

**Agent 02: Exposure Monitor**
- Role: AGGREGATE · REPORT · ALERT
- Monitors: Cross-platform gross and net exposures, counterparty concentration, sector limits
- Decides: Concentration breach alerts, hedging recommendations, escalation to risk desks
- Controls Respected: Counterparty limits, sector exposure caps, regulatory position limits
- Integrations: ICE, Murex, Reuters

**Agent 03: Journal Entry**
- Role: EXTRACT · CLASSIFY · POST
- Monitors: Bank statement feeds, transaction queues, and unposted item backlogs
- Decides: GL account mapping, entry classification, approval routing for exceptions
- Controls Respected: GL mapping policies, dual-approval thresholds, entity-level posting rules
- Integrations: SAP, Oracle, NetSuite

**Agent 04: Reconciliation**
- Role: VERIFY · MATCH · RESOLVE
- Monitors: Internal ledger positions vs. custodian statements, bank feeds, clearing house records
- Decides: Break classification, auto-resolution for known patterns, escalation for unmatched items
- Controls Respected: Tolerance thresholds, aging limits, mandatory human review for high-value breaks
- Integrations: DTC, SWIFT, Custodian APIs

**Agent 05: Close Accelerator**
- Role: ORCHESTRATE · VALIDATE · CLOSE
- Monitors: Close task completion, open item aging, inter-company eliminations, accrual status
- Decides: Task sequencing, automated accruals, exception routing, close certification triggers
- Controls Respected: Close calendar mandates, materiality thresholds, certification authority levels
- Integrations: BlackLine, Workiva, Oracle EPM

**Agent 06: Regulatory Reporting**
- Role: COMPILE · VALIDATE · SUBMIT

- Decides: Data aggregation logic, schema mapping, validation flags, pre-submission audit review
- Controls Respected: CFTC/SEC mandates, submission windows, jurisdictional data residency rules
- Integrations: CFTC Portal, SEC EDGAR, FCA

**Agent 07: Liquidity Watch**
- Role: FORECAST · MONITOR · ALERT
- Monitors: Intraday cash positions, funding gaps, credit facilities, and settlement obligations
- Decides: Stress scenario triggers, funding source recommendations, liquidity buffer alerts
- Controls Respected: LCR/NSFR minimums, internal liquidity buffers, board-approved stress scenarios
- Integrations: Fed Wire, CHIPS, Treasury TMS

**Agent 08: Billing Validation**
- Role: VALIDATE · FLAG · AUDIT
- Monitors: Complex billing calculations, invoice feeds, and billing rule configurations
- Decides: Validation pass/fail, anomaly flags, escalation to human review
- Controls Respected: Billing policy rules, approval thresholds, entity-level billing mandates
- Integrations: SAP, Oracle, NetSuite, Custom Billing Systems

**Agent 09: JAQ – Junior Accountant**
- Role: DETECT · ANALYZE · EXPLAIN
- Monitors: P&L feeds, variance thresholds, journal entry patterns, and budget vs. actuals
- Decides: Anomaly alerts, natural-language explanation generation, escalation routing
- Controls Respected: Materiality thresholds, entity-level reporting rules, review authorization levels
- Integrations: Oracle EPM, Workiva, SAP, Hyperion
